#How #Agentic #AI Changed the Way I Think About DevOps 😊 👩‍💻 

Week 2 of my DevOps Micro Internship was a significant milestone in my understanding of Agentic AI and modern DevOps workflows.
This week, I moved beyond seeing AI as a simple assistant and started understanding it as an engineering partner capable of supporting structured workflows through tools like Claude Code.

My biggest technical insight was discovering how the Agentic Loop (Gather, Act, Verify) works. I learned that an AI agent does not simply generate answers; it first analyzes context, understands the environment, executes tasks, and validates the results. This approach showed me the importance of context, accuracy, and continuous verification in automation.
I also gained a deeper understanding of CLAUDE.md, Skills, Subagents, MCP, Hooks, and Permissions. These concepts demonstrated how AI workflows can become more reusable, secure, and reliable. In particular, hooks and permissions highlighted the importance of maintaining human control and security boundaries when working with autonomous systems.

On a personal level, I discovered that I am a hands-on learner. I learn best by experimenting, troubleshooting errors, and understanding the reason behind a failure. Working directly with terminal environments helped me become more confident in debugging and managing technical workflows.
The main challenge I identified was moving too quickly without validating my environment first. Small details such as the current directory, configuration files, or required variables can have a major impact on the success of a DevOps workflow.
My improvement system moving forward is simple: before starting any task, I will perform an environment check — verifying my working directory, project structure, and required configurations. This habit will help me reduce unnecessary errors and work more efficiently.

My biggest takeaway from Week 2 is that Agentic AI does not replace DevOps engineers 👩‍💻 ; it enhances their capabilities. The future of DevOps will require engineers who can combine automation, security awareness, and strong problem-solving skills.
A great week of learning, debugging, and building better engineering habits.
